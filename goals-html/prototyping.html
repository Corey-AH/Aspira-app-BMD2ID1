Prototyping

Example

Why?

Prototyping is a key part of the design process because it allows ideas to become visible and testable. Rather than just thinking about how something might work, you can actually experience and improve it. This helps designers make better decisions based on real interactions.

What Is It?

Prototyping can be as simple as sketching on paper or as advanced as building interactive digital mockups. It often includes:
Wireframes basic layouts showing structure
Low-fidelity prototypes quick, rough versions to explore ideas
High-fidelity prototypes detailed designs that closely simulate the final product
User flows visual maps of how people move through a design.
